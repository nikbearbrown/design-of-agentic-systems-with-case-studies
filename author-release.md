# Author Release and Publication Agreement

**Design of Agentic Systems with Case Studies — Spring 2026 Edition**
Publisher: Bear Brown LLC
Editor: Nik Bear Brown

---

## ⚠️ About this document

This is a working draft produced for a book project, **not reviewed by an attorney**. Before using this with actual student contributors, have a lawyer licensed in your jurisdiction review it. Publication agreements involve copyright licensing, distribution terms, and — if any students are under 18 — minor-author considerations that vary by state and by institution. Northeastern University may also have its own policies about student-authored work produced in coursework.

Specific things a lawyer should examine:

- Whether the rights structure should be a **license** (author retains copyright, publisher receives a license to publish — this draft's approach) or an **assignment** (rights transfer to publisher).
- Whether Northeastern's student-work policies apply. Most universities do not claim rights to student coursework output, but confirming avoids surprise later.
- Whether **Kindle Unlimited enrollment** requires specific language about [KDP Select exclusivity](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/GQK9AD3KFKPKWNDK) — Amazon requires that enrolled titles not be distributed digitally elsewhere during the enrollment term. This draft names the exclusivity; the lawyer should confirm the scope is correctly described.
- Whether the release needs to be **witnessed** (usually not, but some institutions prefer it).
- Whether **international students** (F-1, OPT, other statuses) have work-authorization concerns with uncompensated academic publication. Typically not, but confirmation is cheap.

What follows is a complete draft. Strike through or revise per legal advice before use.

## Author Release and Publication Agreement

This agreement is between **Bear Brown LLC**, a Wyoming limited liability company with principal address 30 N Gould St Ste N, Sheridan, WY 82801 (hereafter "Publisher"), and **_______________________________** (hereafter "Author").

The Publisher is producing *Design of Agentic Systems with Case Studies*, Spring 2026 Edition (hereafter "the Book"), edited by Nik Bear Brown (hereafter "the Editor"). The Author has submitted a chapter titled

**_______________________________________________________________**

(hereafter "the Chapter") for inclusion in the Book. The Author and Publisher agree to the following terms.

### 1. The Author retains copyright

The Author retains full copyright ownership of the Chapter. Copyright is **not transferred** to the Publisher. The Author's byline appears on the Chapter in every edition in which the Chapter is published, and the Author may cite, list, and describe the Chapter as their own work in any context, including résumés, academic CVs, portfolios, graduate or job applications, and subsequent scholarly work.

### 2. What the Author grants to the Publisher

The Author grants the Publisher a **non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license** to:

a. Include the Chapter in the Spring 2026 Edition of the Book.
b. Distribute the Book through Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing ("KDP"), including enrollment in the Kindle Unlimited / KDP Select program, which requires that the Book as a whole be distributed digitally only through Amazon during the enrollment term.
c. Make the Chapter available as part of the Book in both paid and promotional distributions (see Section 4).
d. Edit the Chapter for clarity, accuracy, consistency with editorial style, and integration with the Book. Substantive editorial changes will be communicated to the Author before publication, and the Author may withdraw the Chapter before publication if the Author disagrees with editorial changes (see Section 7).
e. Correct factual errors identified after publication via errata.

This license applies only to the Spring 2026 Edition. Subsequent editions of the Book require a new release.

### 3. What the Author does not grant

The Author does **not** grant the Publisher:

a. Rights to include the Chapter in any other book, collection, translation, or derivative work without a separate agreement.
b. Rights to license the Chapter to third parties for separate distribution.
c. Rights to use the Author's name, likeness, or biographical information for marketing purposes beyond the author byline, author page on Amazon, and standard book-marketing contexts (social media announcements of the Book, the Publisher's website listings, press inquiries about the Book).

### 4. Pricing, Kindle Unlimited, and promotional distribution

The Author understands and agrees that:

a. The Book will be sold on Amazon Kindle at the KDP minimum price (currently $0.99 USD; subject to Amazon's terms).
b. The Book will be enrolled in **Kindle Unlimited** through KDP Select for at least the initial enrollment term. During this term, the Book is distributed digitally only through Amazon.
c. The Book will be made available **free of charge** for the maximum number of promotional days Amazon permits under KDP Select during the enrollment term.
d. These distribution and pricing decisions are the Publisher's to make, and the Author consents to them as conditions of the Chapter's inclusion.

### 5. Proceeds from sales

The Author understands and agrees that:

a. The Publisher will receive any royalties or payments from Amazon for sales of the Book.
b. The Publisher will use such proceeds solely to fund future opportunities for future students — including but not limited to honoraria for guest speakers, expenses related to subsequent cohort editions, scholarships or stipends for student contributors to future editions, or other uses directly supporting student participation in this book project or its successors.
c. The Author will **not** receive royalties, advance payments, or other direct financial compensation for the Chapter. The Author's compensation is the publication credit, the author byline, and the right to cite the Chapter as a published work.
d. The Publisher will maintain reasonable records of proceeds and their use, and will make a summary of such use available to past authors on request.

The Author affirms that the consideration named above — publication credit and byline — is sufficient consideration for the license granted in this agreement, and that the Author does not expect, and has not been promised, financial compensation.

### 6. The Author's representations

The Author represents and warrants that:

a. The Chapter is the Author's original work, or, where it includes quoted material, those quotations fall within fair use or are properly licensed or attributed.
b. The Chapter does not knowingly infringe the copyright, trademark, privacy, or publicity rights of any person or entity.
c. The sources cited in the Chapter are accurately represented; factual claims are grounded in primary or verifiable secondary sources.
d. The Chapter does not contain material the Author knows to be false or defamatory.
e. The Author has the right to grant the license in this agreement — meaning the Chapter is not subject to a prior publication agreement or employer claim that would prevent this license.

If the Author is uncertain about any of (a) through (e), the Author will raise the concern with the Editor before signing this agreement.

### 7. Withdrawal and revision

The Author may withdraw the Chapter from the Book at any time before the Publication Date defined in Section 8. Withdrawal is made by written notice to the Editor.

After the Publication Date, the Chapter cannot be removed from the Spring 2026 Edition as sold; the Spring 2026 Edition is a snapshot product, and copies already purchased or borrowed through Kindle Unlimited remain with their readers. The Author may, however, decline to have the Chapter included in any future edition.

If the Editor proposes substantive editorial changes after the Author's submission, the Editor will communicate those changes to the Author and allow the Author to accept them, propose alternatives, or withdraw the Chapter. "Substantive" means changes to the Chapter's argument, factual claims, or attribution — not copy-editing, formatting, or integration with the Book's style.

### 8. Publication date and the release deadline

The Publication Date of the Spring 2026 Edition is **_____________________** (to be filled in by the Publisher).

The Author must deliver a signed copy of this agreement to the Publisher **no later than _______ calendar days before the Publication Date** (the "Release Deadline").

**If the Publisher has not received a signed release from the Author by the Release Deadline, the Chapter will be removed from the Book prior to publication.** The Chapter's removal under this clause does not retroactively affect any course credit the Author earned for the Chapter as a course assignment.

### 9. Corrections and errata

After publication, the Author may submit correction requests for the Chapter to the Editor at any time. Corrections to factual errors will be incorporated into the Book's errata page for the Spring 2026 Edition. Corrections to the Chapter's content itself (beyond errata) may be incorporated into future editions if the Author elects to have the Chapter included in those editions.

### 10. Termination

Either party may terminate this agreement before the Publication Date by written notice to the other party. After the Publication Date, this agreement remains in effect for the duration of the Spring 2026 Edition's availability through Amazon. The Publisher may remove the Spring 2026 Edition from sale at any time; doing so does not require the Author's consent.

### 11. No employment relationship

The Author is not an employee, contractor, agent, or partner of the Publisher. This agreement creates no employment relationship, no obligation by the Publisher to provide future work or compensation to the Author, and no obligation by the Author to produce further work for the Publisher.

### 12. Entire agreement

This agreement is the complete and exclusive agreement between the parties regarding the Chapter and the Spring 2026 Edition. Any changes to this agreement must be made in writing and signed by both parties.

### 13. Governing law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Wyoming, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising under this agreement will be resolved in the state or federal courts located in Sheridan County, Wyoming.

## How this document is used in the workflow

The Release Deadline in Section 8 is enforced by the TA workflow. A chapter whose author has not returned a signed copy of this agreement by the deadline is removed from the Book before publication. This is stated explicitly in the README's "For contributors" section so students see it before they invest in writing.

Signed releases are stored by the Publisher. The original file lives with Bear Brown LLC's records (not in the GitHub repository). A log of which chapters have signed releases — with author name, chapter title, and date received — is maintained in `ta-workflow/releases-received.md` as a tracking document, but the signed copies themselves are not committed to the repo.
